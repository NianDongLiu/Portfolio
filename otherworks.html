Simple Design

AI-Driven Narrative Development Tool

Simple Design is an innovative AI-assisted narrative development tool designed to streamline the process of creating game narratives and storyboards. Similar to Twine but enhanced with AI-powered writing assistance, this tool helps game designers and writers manage complex branching narratives more efficiently. The platform integrates artificial intelligence to suggest dialogue options, plot developments, and narrative branches while maintaining an intuitive visual interface for organizing story structures. Simple Design bridges the gap between traditional narrative design tools and modern AI capabilities, making it easier for creators to develop rich, interactive storytelling experiences without getting lost in the complexity of branching paths.

Starship Delivery Robots Research

User Experience Research and Redesign

This UX research project investigated Oregon State University students' experiences with Starship delivery robots through interviews, observations, and secondary research. The team identified common issues including spilled drinks, routing problems, sidewalk obstruction, and accessibility concerns. Through user interviews and behavioral observations, the research explored students' attitudes toward the robots and investigated potential alternative uses beyond food delivery, such as scavenger hunts, photography services, and campus events. The project employed UX design methodologies including scenario development, storyboarding, and user interviews to propose innovative solutions that could enhance the robots' value on campus while addressing current pain points.

In-Person Shopping Experience Enhancement

Incentivizing In-Person Shopping: The Free Popcorn Solution

This research project examined the declining trend of in-person shopping and proposed a solution to incentivize customers to visit physical stores through promotional offerings, specifically free popcorn. The team conducted user interviews to understand shopping preferences, analyzed the financial feasibility of implementing free snack programs in major retailers like Walmart, and developed a socio-technical analysis of how such programs would impact customer behavior and store operations. The research included detailed cost-benefit analysis showing that offering free popcorn would cost approximately 0.22-0.33% of Walmart's annual profit while potentially increasing foot traffic and customer satisfaction. The project utilized GOMS analysis, task analysis, and scenario-based design methodologies to demonstrate the practical implementation of this concept.

Theater & Performance

Acting & Script Adaptation | Stage Design

In this theatrical production, I portrayed the character Tiejiang while simultaneously serving as co-writer and contributing to script adaptation and stage lighting design. This dual role provided invaluable insights into narrative construction and spatial storytelling—skills that directly translate to my game design practice. Working closely with the director and cast, I learned how to craft immersive experiences that engage audiences emotionally and intellectually. The process of blocking scenes, designing lighting cues to enhance dramatic moments, and understanding audience psychology taught me the delicate balance between player agency and directed experience. This theatrical background deeply informs my approach to environmental storytelling, pacing, and creating emotionally resonant game moments. The experience reinforced my belief that games, like theater, are fundamentally about creating meaningful connections between the creator's vision and the audience's experience.
